Experience 7 - 11/06/2020

Team Project - Capture The Flag Project, Cybersecurity

In the Capture The Flag Project, you had a team of people who were assigned to hack into a specialized website to reveal personal information that the company help, or to access parts of the website that a new user normally couldn't access. We also got to learn about the way hackers set up their plan of attack on websites and databases. Much like your average robbery, the hackers have to figure out where they want to attack, scope out that location, develop a weapon like a virus or phishing email (an email sent that can steal the recipients data depending on what they do), deply that weapon, and successfully get away with the stolen goods. Hackers may call for a ransom to get that data deleted as well. Then, during the actual project, we learned a bit about Linux, a good OS for hacking without being tracked, and also about some programs that can read requests. We also learned about code injections and some other useful ways to get through vulnerable parts of software. It was a very fun project, and was a great learning experience too.

Experience 5 - 10/06/2020

Guest Speaker - Gino Capito, TeleComp

When Guest Speaker Gino Capito came to Ignite today, he shared some interesting messages. He talked about his experiences in the technology field, and shared a lot of interesting knowledge about WiFi. He mentioned that almost all resedential IP addresses start with 192.168, and that a bunch of business IP addresses start with 10, which I thought was interesting. He also talked about how jobs tend to hire more on soft skills than hard skills. For example, if he two people apply for a job, and one had signifigantly more soft skills than the other, and the other had signifigantly more hard skills than the other, he'd hire the one with more soft skills. He said that was because hard skills are a lot easier to teach to someone than hard skills. It was nice to hear that, because now I know that soft skills are pretty much essential for the workplace.

Experience 4 - 09/30/2020

Guest Speaker - Mike Meadors, Bomb Squad

When Guest Speaker Mike Meadors came to Ignite today, he shared some interesting messages. He talked about his experiences being in the Bomb Squad, and the tools they have access to. He shared that he was one of about 2200 people in the US that worked in as a member of the Bomb Squad. He had this job for quite a while, and has had access to some very cool tools. One of those being a remote controlled robot that they would use. The robot had a lot of cool features, like a gun mounted on the front, a controllable arm that was about 12 feet long, the ability to climb up many sorts of slopes, and cameras on different sides of the robot. There were some other features, but those were the main ones (as well as the most interesting). He also got to use a bomb suit, which added on to his weight by at least 90lbs. The bomb suit had ceramic inside of it to protect, and also buttons on the arm to access communications and such. Those were the most interesting things about the visit from the Bomb Squad.

Experience 3 - 09/23/2020

Guest Speaker - Tyler Orsak, Art Director with Prisma

When Guest Speaker Tyler Orsak came to Ignite today, he shared some interesting messages. He talked about his experiences in the art and web design field. He said he started as a freelance designer, which means he worked for himself and took jobs when people needed him. One thing I thought was interesting was that he said communication is key. He said communication is vital to a civilization's growth, and he wasn't wrong. He mentioned that if you have good communication with your clients, they'll be more likely to refer to you again if they ever need your help again, or they might tell their friends about your abilities, which will lead to you getting more jobs.

Experience 2 - 09/21/2020

Guest Speaker - Adam Holland, Cyber Security

When Guest Speaker Adam Holland came to Ignite today, he shared some interesting messages. He shared his experiences in the security field, both physical and cyber forms. One thing he said was that back when he was just starting out in Cyber Security, hacking and stealing info was a lot easier for a normal person than it is now, which I thought was interesting. Something else he noted was that the reason big TVs have gone so far down in price in the last decade was because it is so easy to take your personal data from when you are using your TV nowadays, and your data is very valuable to advertising companies. I didn't know that, so that was cool to learn. I had always thought TVs went down in price just because the technology was easier to access.
